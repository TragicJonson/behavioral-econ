We incorrectly predict our ability to complete tasks depending on their level of difficulty. It suggests that we are overly confident in how successful we will be at hard tasks and under-confident about how successful we will be at easy ones. "Experts who make a living 'commenting or offering advice on political and economic trends' were not 'better than journalists or attentive readers of the *New York Times* in 'reading' emerging situations."
